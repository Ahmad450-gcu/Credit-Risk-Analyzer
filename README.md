# Private Ledger

Private Ledger is a credit risk assessment tool built with Streamlit and powered by an XGBoost machine learning model. It takes a customer's financial profile as input and returns a probability of default, giving analysts a fast, structured way to evaluate credit risk without relying on gut feel or manual spreadsheet work.

---

## What It Does

The application accepts ten financial indicators from the user, runs them through a trained XGBoost classifier, and outputs a default probability score between 0 and 1. The result is displayed with a visual risk gauge and a color-coded risk tier — Low, Moderate, or High — so the outcome is immediately readable at a glance.

---

## Input Fields

The model uses the following ten features to generate its prediction.

**Liquidity and Exposure**

Revolving Utilization represents the percentage of unsecured credit currently in use. A value close to 1 indicates the customer is near their credit limit, which is one of the strongest signals of elevated risk in the model.

Debt Ratio is the ratio of monthly debt payments to monthly gross income or assets. Values under 0.35 are generally considered healthy.

**Capacity Metrics**

Age captures the applicant's age, accepted between 18 and 110.

Dependents is the number of financial dependents in the household.

Monthly Income is the gross monthly income in USD. This field supports imputation internally, so leaving it at zero will not break the prediction, though providing an accurate figure will yield a more reliable result.

**Historical Integrity**

30 to 59 Days Past Due, 60 to 89 Days Past Due, and 90 or More Days Past Due each represent the number of times a payment fell into that delinquency window over the past 24 months. Even a single entry in the 90-plus field has a significant impact on the predicted risk score.

Open Credit Lines is the total number of active installment and revolving credit lines.

Real Estate Loans is the number of mortgages or home equity lines currently held.

---

## How the Model Works

The prediction pipeline consists of four steps. First, flagged outlier values (96 and 98) in the past-due fields are treated as encoding errors and replaced with zero. Second, missing values in income and dependents are imputed using a pre-fitted imputer. Third, the revolving utilization, debt ratio, and income fields are capped at their 99th percentile values from training data to prevent extreme outliers from distorting the output. Fourth, all features are scaled using a pre-fitted standard scaler before being passed to the XGBoost classifier.

The final output is the probability assigned to class 1 (default), expressed as a decimal between 0 and 1.

## Risk Tiers

| Score Range  | Tier          |
| ------------ | ------------- |
| 0.00 to 0.50 | Low Risk      |
| 0.51 to 0.75 | Moderate Risk |
| 0.76 to 1.00 | High Risk     |

## Project Structure

```
private_ledger/
    app.py                        Main Streamlit application
    best_xgboost_model.pkl        Trained XGBoost classifier
    imputer.pkl                   Fitted SimpleImputer for missing values
    scaler.pkl                    Fitted StandardScaler
    feature_columns.pkl           Ordered list of feature names used during training
    cap_values.pkl                Per-feature cap thresholds (99th percentile)
```

## Important Notes

Predictions generated by this tool are based on historical training data and statistical patterns. They are intended to support human decision-making, not replace it. This output does not constitute a final credit decision and should always be reviewed in the context of additional qualitative information about the applicant.
